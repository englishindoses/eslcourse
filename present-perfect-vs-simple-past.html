Present Perfect vs. Simple Past

Warm-up Activity | Intermediate Level | 15 minutes

Quick Grammar Explanation

We use the Present Perfect (have/has + past participle) when describing experiences or actions that connect the past to the present, or when the time is not specified. We also use it for unfinished time periods and recent actions with present relevance. We use the Simple Past (verb + ed/irregular form) when describing completed actions at a specific time in the past.

Present Perfect

"I have visited Paris." (sometime in my life, no specific time)

"She has worked here for five years." (started in the past, still continues)

"I have already eaten." (within an unfinished time period, today)

"Have you had lunch?" (asking about the current day)

Simple Past

"I visited Paris last summer." (specific time in the past)

"She worked there from 2010 to 2015." (completed time period)

"I ate lunch at 1pm." (specific time)

"Did you go to the meeting yesterday?" (specific past time)

Present Perfect in Work Contexts

The Present Perfect is commonly used in professional settings to discuss recent accomplishments, ongoing projects, and current status updates.

"I have finished the presentation." (ready now)
"She has sent the email to the client." (just happened)
"Have you received my report?" (asking about current status)
"We have met our quarterly targets." (recent achievement)
"The team has been working on this project for months." (ongoing)
"I haven't had time to review your proposal yet." (until now)

Interactive Activity: Choose the Correct Tense

For each situation, decide whether to use Present Perfect or Simple Past. Explain your choice.

  1. John: __________ (you / ever / eat) sushi?
    Mary: Yes, I __________ (try) it for the first time last month.
  2. Manager: __________ (you / finish) the quarterly report yet?
    Employee: Yes, I __________ (complete) it this morning. I __________ (email) it to you an hour ago.
  3. Tourist: __________ (you / visit) the Tower of London?
    Local: Yes, I __________ (go) there three times in my life. In fact, I __________ (take) my children there last weekend.
  4. Interviewer: __________ (you / work) on any similar projects before?
    Candidate: Yes, I __________ (manage) a team of five developers at my previous company. We __________ (deliver) three major applications last year.
  5. Colleague 1: __________ (you / have) lunch yet?
    Colleague 2: No, I __________ (not / have) time. I __________ (be) in meetings all morning, and I __________ (just / finish) the client proposal.

Conversation Questions

Use these questions to have a natural conversation. Try to use both Present Perfect and Simple Past in your answers.

  1. Have you already had lunch today? What did you eat the last time you went to a restaurant?
  2. Have you finished any important tasks at work/school recently? When did you complete them and how did you feel afterward?
  3. Have you tried any new hobbies or activities this year? How did you become interested in them?
  4. Have you ever met anyone famous? When did this happen and what was the experience like?
  5. Have you noticed any changes in your work/study habits since the pandemic? When did these changes begin and which ones have you maintained?

This activity is designed to help intermediate students practice and differentiate between Present Perfect and Simple Past tenses in natural conversation, with special focus on unfinished time frames and professional contexts.
